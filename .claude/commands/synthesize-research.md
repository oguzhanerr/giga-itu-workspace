# /synthesize-research

Convert raw research (interview notes, survey data, feedback) into structured insights.

## Input
The user provides or pastes: interview notes, survey responses, user feedback, or a mix. Specify the product or theme if relevant.

## Output
A research synthesis saved to `2_for-review/research-synthesis-<topic>-<date>.md` with:

- **Key themes** — patterns that appeared across multiple sources
- **User needs** — what users are trying to accomplish (jobs to be done)
- **Pain points** — friction, frustrations, and failures
- **Opportunities** — gaps where a product solution could help
- **Quotes** — 2–3 verbatim quotes that best illustrate the findings
- **Open questions** — things the research didn't answer

## Rules
- Synthesise, don't summarise — extract patterns, not a list of what each person said
- Tie insights back to product implications where clear
- Flag thin evidence (single data point) vs strong signal (multiple sources)
- Save to `2_for-review/` — user reviews before acting on findings
